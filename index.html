Subbaiah T.S.

Co Founder Bioman Technologies

Subbaiah uses his design thinking skills to develop solutions to solve urban issues. His firm belief in the power of democracy led him to start Citizens for Sustainability (CIFOS) an NGO that works on building models where citizens are empowered to solve urban issues. Bioman technologies which he has co-founded with Joseph Fernandes is a model that proves how urban waste issue can be solved in a sustainable way by citizens. Subbaiah is a SSE India Fellow 2018 & Fellow of The Indian Institute of Architects (IIA)and BCLIP Fellow 2013.

Hoping to solve all social problems by becoming a politician, Subbaiah ran for the office of the Corporator ward 18 of BBMP in 2015 as an Independent candidate. Even though he lost the election he continued to work on several solutions that could solve the problems in his neighbourhood. He started the Ward level Micro Plan for Solid Waste Management (SWM) for BBMP when Mr.Subodh Yadav was the Special Commissioner for SWM at BBMP .His trust CIOFS hosted the first Composting Santhe a community event to promote various composting technologies and promote segregation of waste at home.

The BBMP went on to make it a feature in every ward. Infact Bioman first launched their product commercially at the very first Compost Santhe in Feb 2017. He also worked towards Plastic Ban as a policy in the city. He has conducted several social awareness programs such as Cleanliness drive, Voters enrolment Drive, Kill Bill - Anti Posters and Flex drive, Footpath clearance drive etc. He has been actively involved in conducting Cycle Day Sanjaynagar and promoting cycling in the neighbourhoods and spreading the same to many communities.

Graduate of 1st batch of Bangalore Civic Leadership Incubation Program (BCLIP) a flagship project of by BPAC. He has a formal degree in Architecture from M.S.Ramaiah Institute of Technology. He also holds a Master’s Degree in Design from the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore (IISc). He is also pursuing a master’s in urban planning from the Institute of Town Planning New Delhi.
